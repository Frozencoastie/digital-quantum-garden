# Sustainable Living in Alaska

Living sustainably in Alaska isn't about importing Southern California's solar-panel aesthetic. It's about understanding place, respecting limits, and building resilience in a landscape that doesn't forgive shortcuts.
>[!note]
>If we choose simplicity then such be our lives
>
## What Sustainability Means Here

In a state where:
- Winter darkness lasts 20 hours a day
- Supply chains are fragile and expensive
- Heating costs can break you
- The land itself is the greatest teacher

Sustainability becomes deeply practical, not theoretical.

## Core Principles

### Energy Consciousness
We don't have the luxury of wastefulness. Every kilowatt-hour in winter is precious. This has taught me more about [[Living Within Limits\|Living Within Limits]] than any environmental philosophy book.

See also: [[The 100-Mile Diet in Alaska\|The 100-Mile Diet in Alaska]] - spoiler: it's challenging.
