*Where embodied knowing takes root and ideas cross-pollinate across orchards of thought*

I'm exploring what happens when we refuse the Western bifurcation of mind/body, soul/psyche, sacred/secular. When we return to **Nephesh** - the Hebrew understanding of the integrated human being.

# Why "Digital Garden" Over "Digital Orchard"

## Practical Reasons

1. **"Garden" is the established terminology** in the digital garden movement
2. **More discoverable** - people search "digital garden" not "digital orchard"
3. **Flexibility** - Gardens can contain orchards, but orchards can't contain gardens
4. **Metaphorical range** - Gardens include more than just fruit production

## Conceptual Reasons

1. **Garden = sacred space** (Eden, Gethsemane, Song of Solomon)
2. **Garden = integration** (cultivated + wild, known + unknown)
3. **Garden = Nephesh** (the whole ecology, not just the productive parts)
4. **Orchard = subset** (one type of cultivation within the larger garden)

## Your Vision Specifically

You said: **"This Garden will have multiple Orchards"**

That sentence structure itself suggests:
- **Garden** = the whole
- **Orchards** = the parts
